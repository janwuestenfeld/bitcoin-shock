Bitcoin Conditional SHOCK Regime Classifier

Not all macro shocks are good for Bitcoin. This dashboard shows when shocks matter for BTC vs SPY over the next 60 days — conditional on the macro regime.

Developed by Dr. Jan Wuestenfeld —

Bitcoin's structural properties — non-sovereign, fixed supply, no counterparty — make it a natural candidate for safe-haven flows during macro and geopolitical shocks. The Bitcoin-native investment narrative leans on this: macro stress should be a tailwind. The historical record is more nuanced. Banking-stress events do behave like a true reversal catalyst, consistent with the safe-haven thesis. But oil, dollar, and rates shocks terminate BTC uptrends rather than supporting them, and geopolitical-threat shocks impose a uniform −5pp drag regardless of direction. The shock-as-tailwind story holds for one channel and partially fails for three others — and which channel fires today determines whether BTC outperforms SPY over the next 60 days.

The dashboard pattern-matches today's macro state — VIX, era, active shocks, BTC prior 60d momentum — against 84 historical analogue cells, and reports what BTC actually did vs SPY in those analogues. It tells you when the safe-haven thesis applies, when a shock is a trend-killer instead, and when there's no edge in either direction.

Method. Walk-forward identification (no look-ahead). +1.97pp OOS R² beyond BTC's own momentum and a macro-regime baseline. n=2,818 trading days, ridge + 5-fold CV, permutation-tested. A structured prior over the 60d return distribution — not a price target, not a buy/sell signal.

One-line economic intuition per shock. A shock fires when its raw measure crosses the top-decile threshold of its within-era distribution — computed walk-forward (no look-ahead). Each shock transmits through a different macro channel.

    Worked examples: when shocks helped, hurt, or did nothing

    Four historical events showing the framework's regime-conditional thesis in action. The naive "shocks help BTC" narrative would predict all four were bullish — the framework correctly identifies one trend-killer (COVID, the framework was blind to the Fed reaction), one calibrated no-edge call (Russia-Ukraine), and two clean bullish setups (US-Iran 2020, SVB 2023). The variation across these four episodes is the dashboard's point.

    🟢 US-Iran escalation · 3 Jan 2020 · HIGH-BETA-BULL

    Setting. VIX 14.0 (calm), pre-COVID, no shocks firing on the framework's thresholds, BTC +25% prior 60d.

    Classification. Calm tape, positive momentum, no offsetting macro headwind — the textbook HIGH-BETA-BULL cell. Comparable cells historically delivered ~70% share-positive at h=60d.

    Realized. BTC +20%, SPY −7%, outperformance +27pp.

    ✓ Clean hit. Geopolitical noise into a calm-VIX uptrend resolves into the trend, not against it. Realized print at the upper end of the cell distribution.

    🟡 COVID outbreak · 9 Mar 2020 · TREND-KILL

    Setting. VIX 54.5 (extreme), oil-price-war + rates + banking shocks all firing simultaneously, BTC flat-to-down prior 60d.

    Classification. Canonical TREND-KILL cell — oil+rates+banking is the historical pattern that broke risk-asset uptrends in 2008, 2011, 2015–16, 2018. Framework would have flagged a hostile environment, share-positive expectation below 50%.

    Realized. BTC +25%, SPY +7%, outperformance +18pp.

    ⚠️ Instructive miss. The framework was blind to the Fed's $3tn balance-sheet expansion. BTC re-rated as a liquidity asset, not a risk asset. TREND-KILL captures the shock; it does not capture the central-bank reaction function. Documented limitation.

    🔵 Russia-Ukraine invasion · 21 Feb 2022 · DRAG

    Setting. VIX 28 (elevated), GPR-threat firing hard on invasion print, oil not yet at threshold, BTC −35% from Nov 2021 high. Prior 60d: negative.

    Classification. DRAG cell — GPR firing with BTC trending down. Comparable cells deliver share-positive near 50%, tight distribution centered near zero. The framework would have called this "no edge, expect noise around zero" — explicitly not a directional view.

    Realized. BTC +7%, SPY −2%, outperformance +8.7pp.

    ✓ Calibrated hit. Realized print at the 73rd percentile of the DRAG distribution — bullish tail draw, but inside the cell. This is the headline lesson: a naive "war = sell risk" prior would have been wrong; a naive "BTC = safe haven" prior would have overshot. The framework correctly described a no-edge tape.

    🟢 SVB regional banking · 9 Mar 2023 · REVERSAL-PRIMED

    Setting. VIX 25 climbing, banking-stress spiking (KBW Bank Index drawdown, FRA-OIS, regional bank CDS), oil/dollar quiet, BTC recovering off FTX lows with +35% prior 60d.

    Classification. The framework's designated reversal catalyst — REVERSAL-PRIMED. Banking-system stress is expected to redirect flow into BTC as a counterparty-free alternative. Comparable cells delivered ~75% share-positive, median in the mid-twenties.

    Realized. BTC +36%, SPY +6%, outperformance +30pp.

    ✓ Canonical mechanism. The Fed's BTFP backstop on March 12 confirmed banking-system fragility precisely as the reversal channel predicts. This is the cleanest demonstration of the banking-reversal mechanism in the post-2020 sample, and it drives a meaningful share of the +1.97pp OOS R² edge.
